[DRAFT] CSV on the Web Drafts is a W3C Candidate Recommendation (Call for Implementations)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Dear Advisory Committee Representative,
Chairs,

I am pleased to announce that the following documents are now Candidate Recommendations:

Model for Tabular Data and Metadata on the Web
	http://www.w3.org/TR/2015/CR-tabular-data-model-20150716/

Metadata Vocabulary for Tabular Data
	http://www.w3.org/TR/2015/CR-tabular-metadata-20150716/

Generating RDF from Web Tabular Data
	http://www.w3.org/TR/2015/CR-csv2rdf-20150716/

Generating JSON from Web Tabular Data
	http://www.w3.org/TR/2015/CR-csv2json-20150716/


The approval and publication are in response to this transition request:
   	https://lists.w3.org/Archives/Member/chairs/2015JulSep/0006.html

The disposition of comments since the last Working Draft is available at:
	https://github.com/w3c/csvw/issues?utf8=%E2%9C%93&q=label%3A%22For+LCCR%22+-label%3A%22Use+Case+Document%22+

The group welcomes feedback by email to its public mailing list <public-csv-wg-comments@w3.org>, or as issues raised in:
	https://github.com/w3c/csvw/issues

A document with test cases, how to test, etc. is at:
	http://www.w3.org/2013/csvw/tests/

Patent disclosures relevant to these specifications may be found on the CSVW  Working Group's patent disclosure page in conformance with W3C policy:
    https://www.w3.org/2004/01/pp-impl/68238/status

The CSS Working Group expects to satisfy its implementation criteria by 30 October 2015.

This Call for Implementations follows section 7.4 of the W3C Process Document:
    http://www.w3.org/2014/Process-20140801/#candidate-rec

Thank you,

For Tim Berners-Lee, Director, and
Ralph Swick, INK Domain Lead;
Coralie Mercier, Acting Head of W3C Marketing & Communications
